# ADR-137 — Audio direction and localization gate

Audio direction explicitly chooses narrative-led, music-led or hybrid priority. A versioned plan owns beat grid, sections, events, transitions, ducking, overrides and mix/master targets. Protected events resist local overrides. Sound assets carry technical metadata and rights; hybrid retrieval filters rights before ranking.

SFX density, repetition, cooldown and intensity are budgeted before render. Mix automation ducks music under speech, preserves tails/crossfades and targets destination loudness/true peak. The audiovisual critic localizes masking, drift, clipping, pumping, tail and beat issues and distinguishes hard failures from calibrated warnings.

F5 is approved only when PT-BR, EN and ES each own audio, alignment, timeline, subtitles, CTA and localized visual assets; consent permits one lip-sync/avatar case and forces subtitles-only in another; 9:16 and 16:9 outputs pass narrative/hybrid mixes and music-led speech integrity.
