# ADR-136 — Localization variants and timing

Approved canonical ScriptBlocks retain intent, protected claims, qualifiers, CTA, dependencies and source ranges. Each locale owns script version, audio, alignment, plan, formats, commands, jobs and approval while sharing immutable masters. Canonical updates rebase each locale explicitly.

Localized audio duration drives a new alignment and timeline reflow; speech is never blindly stretched to the source. Authorized TTS, local voice, upload, lip-sync, regenerated avatar and subtitles-only are selected through profile, consent, locale and provider capabilities. Locale profiles govern glossary, typography, RTL and formatting. Visible-text derivatives preserve background/lineage and undergo a localized critic.

Music-led montage may govern cuts only with confident beat analysis and never through protected speech boundaries; uncertain beat falls back to narrative-led direction.
